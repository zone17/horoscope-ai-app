## Editorial reading of the data (added by hand)

The auto-recommendation (Sonnet 4.6) is the right call. Three findings shape the read of the data.

### 1. The incumbent isn't suboptimal — it's structurally wrong for the moat

`gpt-4o-mini` scored 1 or 2 on the **antiBarnum** axis in **35 of 36 readings**. That's not a quality regression to fix with prompt-tuning; that's a model that emits Barnum-style universal statements as its default register. Every Sonnet and Opus reading scored 4 or higher on antiBarnum (no 1-2s). Haiku also stays clean (only 2 cells in 1-2). The product positioning is "philosophy that meets you where you are" — generic universal statements are the explicit anti-positioning. Continuing to ship `gpt-4o-mini` is a positioning failure regardless of cost.

### 2. Anti-template is fully solved at Sonnet / Opus

Sonnet and Opus both scored a perfect **5.00 mean on antiTemplate**, with **5/5 on every single reading** (72/72). Haiku came in at 4.91 (31 of 34 cells perfect). Banned words and astrology-template tropes are simply not produced by frontier models when the prompt prohibits them.

**This has direct implications for Phase 1e:** the original parent plan called for self-critique covering anti-cliché + anti-Barnum + anti-template + quote verification. The data says critique should focus solely on **antiBarnum** (means 3.74-4.03 with one perfect 5/5 across all 142 successful Anthropic cells — the remaining wedge) and possibly **voiceAuthenticity tail cases** (Haiku/Sonnet/Opus all bottom out at sagittarius and gemini in the 3.5-4.0 range). Anti-template critique is wasted compute. The parent plan amendment in this PR already reflects this.

### 3. Sonnet and Opus are functionally tied on quality; cost decides

Per-axis "perfect 5" counts (out of 36 successful runs per model):

| Axis | Haiku 4.5 (n=34) | Sonnet 4.6 (n=36) | Opus 4.7 (n=36) |
|---|---|---|---|
| voiceAuthenticity | 5 | 13 | 13 (tie) |
| antiBarnum | 0 | 0 | 1 |
| antiTemplate | 31 | 36 | 36 (tie) |
| quoteFidelity | 27 | 24 | 26 |
| overall | 4 | 11 | 12 |

Sonnet and Opus deliver "would gladly ship" readings at indistinguishable rates (~31% vs ~33% — within run-to-run variance). The two are tied on voiceAuthenticity and antiTemplate; Opus has a 1-cell edge on antiBarnum and overall. Calling Opus meaningfully better than Sonnet on this dataset would be reading noise as signal.

Haiku 4.5 is a real fallback option — its overall mean (4.03) crosses the "would ship" threshold and it's fastest of the three. Worth eval'ing as the cron-path model after Phase 1d corpus work lands, since corpus may close part of Haiku's voice gap.

### Schema flakiness signal

Haiku 4.5 produced 2 judge-fails in 36 cells (5.6%) — both `No object generated: response did not match schema`. V1 had 1 in 36 (2.8%). Across runs the pattern is consistent: Haiku-judging-Haiku is the cross-family combination most prone to schema flake. For Phase 1e (where judge is invoked at runtime in a critique loop), recommend a single retry on schema mismatch before falling back to "no critique applied" — the verb itself stays atomic; the retry policy lives at the call site.

## Methodology

- Date pinned to `2026-04-25` to keep the format-template assignment constant across rows.
- All four models routed through Vercel AI Gateway via `generateObject` + a Zod schema mirroring `ReadingOutput`. This is the same JSON-mode mechanism PR C will adopt for `reading:generate`, so the eval is apples-to-apples and pre-validates that all four models comply with the schema.
- `reading:judge` (Haiku 4.5, temperature 0.2) scores each reading on five axes against the project's codified criteria (sign-profile voice + avoidPatterns, banned words, astrology-template tropes, Barnum patterns, quote register).
- Validators in `reading:generate` (quote-bank fallback, self-match filter) are bypassed — the eval judges raw model output, not post-call cleanup.
- Raw rows committed at `docs/evals/2026-04-25-baseline.json`.
